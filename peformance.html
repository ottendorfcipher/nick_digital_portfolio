Nick, as a performer


When I was 10 years old, I was inspired to learn to play the cello after listening to my dad play "The Liberty Bell" in my parents bedroom.

When I started my own musical journey, I started learning in my school elementary orchestra program. When I turned 13, I took private lessons with Julia Preston, a cellist in the Louisville Orchestra. I was privileged to have such extraordinary teachers, such as Julia Preston, Marsha Curtis-Jones and Brian Powell. These teachers helped me find success.

After graduating high school, I studied with Paul York at the University of Louisville and earned a Bachelor's of Music Education in 2016. I student-taught with Penelope Quesada at Lincoln Performing Arts School and Fred Speck at Eastern High School. I then taught string orchestra at Stuart Academy and Iroquois High School prior to becoming the orchestra director at Fairdale High School in Louisville, Kentucky.

I'm an actively performing cellist in the Louisville-Lexington area and have a passion for teaching kids of all backgrounds and abilities how to play music.
