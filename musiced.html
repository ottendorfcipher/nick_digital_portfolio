Nick, as an educator

I became a High School orchestra director, in part, by the work of teachers and professionals who pushed me to excel in musical and academic endeavors. I believe the qualities instilled in me are valuable to all children, particularly my students at Fairdale High School. I was first exposed to these qualities by my parents. My father, a professional cellist, and my mother, a K-12 general music teacher, trained me to be resilient and persistent in the face of adversity. Their support impressed upon me excites me to teach my own students the value of resiliency when they make mistakes or face the many challenges they encounter daily.

As a string educator, my background is in pedagogy and instruction, having first earned a Bachelor’s in Music Education from the University of Louisville. While in my undegraduate degree, I had the opportunity and privilege to study privately with professors specializing in all stringed orchestral instruments. With their help, I gained a strong working knowledge of the pedagogy behind developing good physical instrumental techniques with children of all ages and abilities. I’ve been able to apply my education to improving student performance daily.

As a result of growing up in Jefferson County, I’ve been able to collaborate with many orchestra teachers in the district. I am honored to be able to call many district orchestra directors not only professional colleagues but close friends. I feel that music is a life-long journey. In an ever-changing, fast-paced society that values instant gratification, I truly believe that music plays a pivotal role in grounding us to what is important and beautiful. I feel blessed to work alongside such an incredible team of committed, passionate educators and I feel that we have an opportunity to impact so many students’ lives in a positive, uplifting way.
